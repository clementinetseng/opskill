## 1. Regulatory Authority: PAGCOR
**PAGCOR** (Philippine Amusement and Gaming Corporation) regulates all gaming activities.
* **Dual Role**: Historically an operator and regulator. Plans are underway to privatize casinos by 2025 to become a pure regulator.
* **Revenue Goal**: Mandated to generate funds for nation-building.

## 2. Licensing Frameworks
It is vital to distinguish between Domestic and Offshore licenses.

### A. Onshore (Domestic) - PIGO
* **Target**: Filipino residents (21+ years old).
* **Origin**: "Philippine Inland Gaming Operator" (PIGO) scheme launched in late 2020.
* **Requirement**: Online operations must often be tied to a physical gaming license (e.g., a casino or bingo hall).
* **Examples**: BingoPlus, Solaire Online.

### B. Offshore (International) - POGO / IGL
* **Target**: **Foreigners only**. Strictly prohibited from accepting Filipino players.
* **Status**: Formerly "POGO", rebranded to "Internet Gaming License" (IGL).
* **Policy Shift**: As of late 2023/2024, the government is moving to **phase out** offshore operators due to crime/reputational risks.

## 3. Critical Compliance Checkpoints

<CheckItem id="reg-age" severity="high">
**Age Verification**: Onshore players must be **21+** (Casino) or **18+** (Bingo/Lottery). Strict KYC is required.
</CheckItem>

<CheckItem id="reg-geo" severity="high">
**Geofencing**: Offshore licensees (IGLs) must block Philippine IPs. Onshore licensees must ensure players are physically in PH.
</CheckItem>

<CheckItem id="reg-banned" severity="critical">
**Banned Verticals**: **E-sabong (Online Cockfighting)** is illegal as of May 2022. Do not offer or promote it.
</CheckItem>

## 4. Responsible Gaming
With the rise of "public health crisis" concerns regarding addiction, PAGCOR is tightening rules:
* Mandatory warning pop-ups.
* Self-exclusion registries.
* Potential bans on influencer marketing for gambling.
